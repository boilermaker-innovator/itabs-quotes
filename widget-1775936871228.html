Why it happens
Most people intend to do the garden on the weekend — but weekends fill up fast. Perth heat makes outdoor work miserable in summer, and before you know it one missed week becomes six. It's not laziness, it's just life.

Overgrown lawns become a haven for snakes, spiders, and bindii. Neglected hedges can become disputes with neighbours. A full garden cleanup costs more the longer you leave it — regular maintenance is almost always cheaper in the long run.

05 / PRICING GUIDE
Honest prices, no hidden extras

Pricing depends on block size, how overgrown things are, and what services you need. The guide below gives you a rough ballpark — but Rick will always confirm a price before starting. No surprises, ever.

Think of it like…
A haircut — a quick tidy costs less than a full cut, style, and wash. A yard that's been maintained is cheaper per visit than one that needs a full reset every time Rick shows up.
Rough price ranges
Standard Mow
A typical suburban block mow (mow, edge, blow down) starts from around $60–$90 depending on block size and grass length. Larger blocks or overgrown lawns will be quoted accordingly before work starts.
Hedging Add-On
Hedging and trimming can be added to a mow visit or booked separately. Simple hedges start from around $30–$60 extra. Larger or more complex hedges are quoted on the spot.
Full Garden Cleanup
One-off full cleanups — including slashing, pruning, weeding, and rubbish removal — are quoted per job based on what's involved. Most standard suburban cleanups range from $200–$500. Rubbish removal is extra if required.
Regular Customer Deals
Customers who book fortnightly or monthly services lock in a consistent rate and often pay less per visit than one-off bookings. Ask Rick about a regular schedule when you get in touch — most people save money by committing to a recurring slot.
